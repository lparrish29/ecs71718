Mohandas Karamchand Ghandi

The Mahatma, Great Soul

Mohandas Karamchand Ghandi was a leader for nationalists in India who were against British rule.


He was known internationally for his protesting in a nonviolent way which helped him gain social and political gain.

Even after his death in 1948, Ghandi grew in fame.

    Some of his accomplishments include;

  1. Fighting against racial discrimination in Africa,
  2. Ending the Indian Relief Act in 1914 with his campaign,
  3. Leading a non-violent tax revolt in Kheda,
  4. Leading the non-cooperation movement in the 1920's,
  5. and leading the salt march to Dandi.
